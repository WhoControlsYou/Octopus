1880s

Chemical Agriculture Foundations

Food & Agriculture Industrial Foundations

The Food & Agriculture Control tentacle (Tentacle 8) takes root as the modern agricultural chemical industry emerges. German chemical companies like BASF transition from producing synthetic dyes to manufacturing fertilizers and pesticides, creating farmer dependency on synthetic inputs that would later evolve into global agribusiness monopolies.

Documented in historical industrial records of BASF, Bayer, and Hoechst
1910

Flexner Report Medical Monopoly

Medical-Pharmaceutical Education Control

The Medical-Pharmaceutical Empire (Tentacle 6) is strategically consolidated with the publication of the Flexner Report, funded by the Carnegie Foundation. This report successfully restructures American medical education to favor allopathic, drug-based treatments while systematically marginalizing holistic, homeopathic, and alternative practices. This centralizes medical power with the entities that would evolve into today's pharmaceutical giants.

Carnegie Foundation archives, Flexner Report 1910
1913

The Federal Reserve Act

Financial Domination Banking Control

Private banking interests establish control over U.S. monetary policy. Passed on December 23rd while most Congress members were away for Christmas. The "Fed" creates money from nothing, charges interest, and controls the economic cycle through boom-and-bust engineering.

Documented in Federal Reserve archives
1913

Standard Oil Breakup & Reconsolidation

Energy & Resource Corporate Control

The Supreme Court orders the breakup of John D. Rockefeller's Standard Oil trust into 34 companies. However, this breakup merely splinters control into regional monopolies (Exxon, Chevron, etc.) that continue to coordinate, cementing Energy & Resource Control (Tentacle 7). Rockefeller retains significant influence over all successor companies, demonstrating how antitrust actions can mask continued centralized control.

Standard Oil Co. of New Jersey v. United States, 221 U.S. 1 (1911)
1931

Political Infiltration False Flag

Japanese military officers stage attack on their own railway in Manchuria, blaming Chinese saboteurs. Provides pretext for full-scale invasion and establishment of puppet state. Early documented false-flag operation with declassified evidence.

Japanese military documents declassified post-WWII
1924

Leaded Gasoline Public Health Experiment

Medical-Pharmaceutical Energy & Resource Public Health

Ethyl Corporation, a joint venture between General Motors and Standard Oil, introduces tetraethyl lead to gasoline despite known health risks. This act of deliberate public health poisoning to protect engine patents showcases the intersection of the Medical and Energy tentacles in prioritizing profit over population health. Lead poisoning would affect generations before being phased out.

Internal GM/Standard Oil memos revealed in later litigation
1933

Gold Confiscation (EO 6102)

Financial Domination Wealth Transfer

Americans forced to surrender gold at $20.67/ounce. Price revalued to $35/ounce days later - a 40% devaluation of savings. Executive Order 6102 criminalized private gold ownership, transferring wealth from citizens to government.
