The UN "Smoking Gun" Report

In 2000, the United Nations Population Division published a landmark report titled "Replacement Migration: Is It a Solution to Declining and Ageing Populations?" This document is the Rosetta Stone of the Great Replacement policy.

  • It explicitly calculates the millions of migrants needed to maintain population size and support the aging dependency ratio in eight countries (France, Germany, Italy, Japan, South Korea, Russia, UK, USA).
  • For the European Union, it projects a need for 1.5 million immigrants per year just to keep the working-age population stable.
  • The report states plainly that migration levels "substantially higher than experienced in the past" would be required, acknowledging this would lead to "significant changes in the ethnic, cultural and religious composition" of host populations.
Source: United Nations, Department of Economic and Social Affairs, Population Division (2000). ST/ESA/SER.A/206. This is a publicly available official UN document.
