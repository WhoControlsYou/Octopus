Mirror & Preserve: Clone This Site

The most effective way to protect this information is through decentralization. Create your own copy (mirror) of this site. If the main site disappears, the network of mirrors ensures the information survives.

1. Download the Complete Site

Use HTTrack or wget to create a local copy of the entire website:

# Using wget (Linux/Mac)
wget --mirror --convert-links --adjust-extension --page-requisites \
  --no-parent https://octopus-control-structures.org
# Using HTTrack (Windows)
# Download HTTrack from httrack.com
# Enter URL: https://octopus-control-structures.org
# Choose "Mirror website" option

2. Choose Hosting

Select a hosting provider (preferably outside Five Eyes jurisdiction):

Free Options

  • GitHub Pages (static hosting)
  • Netlify (free tier)
  • Vercel (free tier)
  • InfinityFree (with limitations)

Privacy-Focused

  • Njalla (anonymous registration)
  • Flokinet (Iceland-based)
  • OrangeWebsite (Iceland)
  • Bahnhof (Sweden)

Onion Services

  • Host as Tor onion service
  • Completely anonymous
  • Requires technical knowledge
  • Instructions: torproject.org

3. Upload & Configure

Upload the downloaded files to your hosting provider:

# Using FTP (replace with your credentials)
ftp your-hosting-provider.com
cd public_html
mput *
quit

Important: Test all links after uploading. Update any hardcoded URLs in the files if necessary.

4. Register a Domain (Optional)

For easier access, register a domain name:

  • Use privacy-focused registrars: Njalla, OrangeWebsite
  • Consider alternative TLDs: .org, .net, .info, .xyz
  • Point DNS to your hosting provider
  • Consider using Cloudflare for DDoS protection (weigh privacy trade-offs)

5. Share Your Mirror

Once your mirror is live, help others find it:

  • Email the main site with your mirror URL (use encrypted email)
  • Share on decentralized platforms: Matrix, Mastodon, Session
  • Include in forum signatures (where appropriate)
  • Add to the distributed mirror list (coming soon)
Operational Security (OPSEC)

Your safety is paramount. Follow these guidelines to protect yourself while engaging in activism:

Use Encrypted Communication: Signal for messaging, ProtonMail or Tutanota for email, Session for anonymous messaging.
Use a VPN: Always connect through a reputable VPN when accessing or mirroring the site. Mullvad and IVPN are privacy-focused options.
Separate Identities: Use different devices/profiles for activism vs. personal life. Consider using Tails OS for high-risk activities.
Know Your Rights: In most countries, distributing factual information is protected speech. Document everything and know when to seek legal counsel.
Work in Groups: Organize with trusted individuals. The "lone wolf" is easier to target than a distributed network.
